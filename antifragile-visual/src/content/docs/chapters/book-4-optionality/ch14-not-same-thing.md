# Chapter 14: When Two Things Are Not the Same Thing

> "The green lumber fallacy: confusing knowledge about a product's price behavior with knowledge about the product itself."

## The Core Insight

A successful lumber trader thought "green lumber" meant painted green, when it actually means freshly cut. His ignorance of this "basic" fact didn't matterâ€”he understood price behavior. This is the **green lumber fallacy**: confusing different types of knowledge, and assuming academic knowledge translates to practical success.
